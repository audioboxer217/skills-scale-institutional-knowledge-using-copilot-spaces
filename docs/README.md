OctoAcme runs projects with an emphasis on iterative delivery, clear artifacts, and structured workflows. Work begins with a lightweight Project One-pager to capture the problem, measurable success metrics, stakeholders, and a high-level timeline, then moves through planning where work is broken into prioritized backlog items with acceptance criteria and estimates. The team uses a project board with columns such as Backlog, Ready, In Progress, In Review, QA, and Done to visualize flow, and enforces a pull request workflow that favors small PRs, links to issues and acceptance criteria, runs CI checks (tests and linting) before review, and requires at least one approval before merging.

Roles and responsibilities are explicitly defined so accountability is clear: Product Managers own outcomes and prioritization, Project Managers coordinate schedules, risks, and communications, Developers implement and test features, and QA/Testing validate acceptance criteria. These personas are used both to assign ownership for artifacts (roadmaps, backlog items, risk register) and to shape team interactions—e.g., product defines success metrics, PMs drive meeting cadence and risk tracking, and developers produce the code and tests that demonstrate Done.

Communication follows a predictable cadence to keep stakeholders aligned and surface issues early: short daily standups focused on progress and blockers, a weekly delivery sync to show progress and flagged risks, PM+PdM weekly alignment, and monthly stakeholder updates. Risk and incident communication is standardized with a Risk Register capturing impact, likelihood, owner, and mitigation, a weekly status template for routine updates, and a three-level escalation path (team → PM → Product Lead → Sponsor) for unresolved or business-impacting blockers. Incident messaging and post-incident retrospectives are also part of the playbook to ensure learning and follow-up.

Quality assurance and release practices are baked into the pipeline. The docs require unit tests for new logic, integration tests where applicable, end-to-end smoke tests for critical flows, security scanning in CI, and manual QA for feature acceptance when needed. Releases follow a defined checklist (pre-release verifications, rollback plans, release notes), distinguish patch/minor/major types, and include post-deploy verifications and an incident playbook for rollbacks. Continuous improvement is enforced through regular retrospectives that convert learnings into prioritized action items tracked in the backlog or risk register.
